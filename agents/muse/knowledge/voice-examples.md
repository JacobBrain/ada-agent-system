### Operations / Systems

**On "working ON the business":**
> For a long time, I misunderstood what "working ON the business, not IN it" really means. I thought it meant finding time unencumbered by distractions, a hot coffee, right after a workout, ready to be creative and think. Sure, there is a time for that. But working ON your business is mostly a shift in thinking.
> - Instead of working on some deliverable, you're taking a bit of extra time to document the process for next time.
> - Instead of rushing off the call after finding a prospect can't pull the trigger yet, you use the opportunity to do some research and figure out more about your buying group.
> - Instead of just shooting a Slack response to a question your team asked, you take 3m and add it to your knowledge base so you don't have to answer it again.
> Don't wait until the stars align to "start working ON your business" â€” Start with that next task.

### AI

**On AI project failures:**
> Most AI projects fail. Often, it's not that "AI isn't ready". It's the business that's not ready. Data lives in 12 different places. Half of it is old. The other half doesn't exist. There are no SOPs for basic processes. New hires spend weeks figuring out what the last person "just knew." The latest formal documentation is from 2019, and more recent examples are in a Slack thread nobody can find. Then people feed this mess to AI and wonder why it doesn't work. AI isn't magic. It's a very talented new employee that gets zero context and superhuman expectations. Fix the boring stuff first.

**On AI teaching leaders:**
> AI is teaching leaders to spec better. I talk to a lot of agencies, and I always discover their leaders really struggle to spec work. No context. Missing details. No end goals. Just words with little real direction. Too often, that gets put at the feet of the people executing the work. Enter AI. These same leaders struggle to get good results with it. Why? Same reasons. No context. Missing details. No desired outcomes. Just words with little real direction. It's literally superhuman, and it doesn't know what it's supposed to do. With AI, there are less excuses, though. Others are getting great results. The difference is the prompt. And the prompter. Give it context. Give it guidance. Give it a desired output. Then do the same for your teams and watch quality rise across the board. Garbage in, garbage out (GIGO)

### Agencies / Scaling

**On scaling expertise:**
> At the end of the day, a professional service firm exists to scale expertise. The founder's experience and knowledge are the product. The game is turning what lives in one head into repeatable value that can be delivered in a systematic way.
