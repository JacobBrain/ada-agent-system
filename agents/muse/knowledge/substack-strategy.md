# Jacob's Substack Strategy & Action Checklist

## Core Positioning

You're the field operator documenting real systems that scale professional services firms ($500K-$5M). No theory, just battle-tested insights from the trenches.

---

## Daily Checklist (30 minutes total)

### Morning Routine (10 minutes)

- [ ]  Follow 3-5 new Substacks in your niche (ops, agencies, AI, business systems)
- [ ]  Check notifications and respond to any comments on your posts
- [ ]  Capture 2-3 Note ideas in your running list

### Content Creation (10 minutes)

- [ ]  Write and publish 1 Note (50-150 words)
    - Operational observation from today
    - System win/failure you witnessed
    - Tool insight or process tweak
    - Client pattern you noticed
    - Question about ops challenges

### Community Engagement (10 minutes)

- [ ]  Apply the 10-5-1 Rule:
    - [ ]  Like 10 relevant Notes/posts
    - [ ]  Leave 5 genuine comments (add value, ask questions)
    - [ ]  Restack 1 piece that your audience would appreciate
- [ ]  Focus on: ops professionals, agency owners, AI implementers, fractional executives

---

## Weekly Checklist (1 hour on Fridays)

### Content Planning (15 minutes)

- [ ]  Review your Note performance from the week
- [ ]  Identify 1-2 high-performing Notes to expand into posts
- [ ]  Batch create 3-5 Notes for next week
- [ ]  Update your content idea bank

### Main Post Creation (45 minutes)

- [ ]  Write and publish your weekly post
- [ ]  Rotate between formats (examples below)
    - **Week 1**: System Breakdown (one operational system step-by-step)
    - **Week 2**: Tool Evaluation (AI/ops tool)
    - **Week 3**: Case Study (anonymized client transformation)
    - **Week 4**: Growth Stage Playbook (challenges at revenue milestones)
- [ ]  End with specific operational question
- [ ]  Include PS with 2-3 newsletter recommendations

### Relationship Building

- [ ]  DM 1 potential collaboration partner
- [ ]  Respond to all comments on your weekly post
- [ ]  Create a "system sharing" thread if applicable
- [ ]  Thank new subscribers personally (if manageable)

---

## Monthly Checklist (2 hours total)

### Month 1 Tasks

- [ ]  Analyze your metrics:
    - Top performing Notes
    - Best engagement times
    - Subscriber growth rate
    - Most active commenters
- [ ]  Update your Substack bio based on what's resonating
- [ ]  Create/update your "Start Here" post for new subscribers

### Month 2+ Tasks

- [ ]  Feature another operator (interview or guest swap)
- [ ]  Host a monthly discussion thread (e.g., "Biggest ops challenge this month?")
- [ ]  Share 2-3 subscriber implementation wins
- [ ]  Review and refine your content pillars
- [ ]  Plan next month's collaboration outreach

### Growth Milestones Review

- [ ]  Month 1 Goal: 50 subscribers
- [ ]  Month 2 Goal: 150 subscribers
- [ ]  Month 3 Goal: 300 subscribers
- [ ]  Adjust strategy based on what's working

## Newsletter & Content Expansion

### When to Start a Newsletter

**Wait until Month 3-4 when you have:**

- 200+ engaged subscribers
- Clear content pillars that resonate
- Enough weekly posts to repurpose
- Systems for consistent delivery

### Newsletter Format Options

1. **The Weekly Operator** - Curated ops insights + your commentary
2. **System of the Week** - Deep dive on one system with templates
3. **Field Notes Digest** - Best of your Notes + subscriber submissions

### Additional Content Types to Test

- **Subscriber Spotlights** - Feature community members' wins
- **Tool Stack Teardowns** - Show your actual tech setup
- **Revenue Milestone Guides** - What breaks at $1M, $2M, $5M
- **Ops Challenges Series** - Crowdsource solutions from community

## Engagement Best Practices

### For Notes

- Hook in first line
- One clear takeaway
- End with "What's your experience with..."
- Use line breaks for mobile reading

### For Posts

- Start with operational observation
- Lead with the system, not theory
- Include implementation steps
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- Bold key insights

### For Comments

- Add value, don't just affirm
- Share relevant experience
- Ask follow-up questions
- Tag other relevant creators

## Remember: You're not building an audience. You're building a community of operators who implement, not just read.
